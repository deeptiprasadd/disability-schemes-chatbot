## Overview
The State Disability Pension is a scheme implemented by the Delhi Social Welfare Department, providing subsistence allowance to persons with disabilities. The scheme (known as Subsistence Allowance/Financial Allowance to persons with special needs) is a fusion of State and Central Government contributions.

## Benefits
- Monthly grant of Rs 2500/-.
- Financial support to persons with disabilities living in Delhi.

## Eligibility criteria
- **Age:** 0 to 60 years (beneficiaries above 60 are automatically transferred to the Old Age Assistance Scheme).
- **Income:** Family income must not exceed Rs. 75,000/- p.a. from all sources.
- **Residence:** Must be a resident of the National Capital Territory of Delhi for at least 5 years preceding the application.
- **Disability:** Minimum 40% disability, certified by a Medical Board of a Govt. Hospital.
- **Banking:** Must have a single-operated account in a Bank or Post Office (relaxations for minors/mentally challenged under Legal Guardianship).

## Required documents
1. **Proof of residence:** (e.g., Ration Card, Voter Card, Passport, Electricity/Water Bill) showing 5 years of residence in Delhi.
2. **Disability certificate:** Issued by a notified Hospital of Govt. of NCT of Delhi or Govt. of India.
3. **Self-declaration:** Regarding family income.
4. **Recommendation:** Verification by a Gazetted Officer or MP/MLA.
5. **Witness Statements:** If no documentary evidence of residence exists, statements from two witnesses (e.g., RWA President, Gazetted Officer, ASHA worker) are required.

## How to apply
- **Offline:** Visit the District Social Welfare Office to obtain and submit the application form.
- **Online:** Forms are available on the official website of the Social Welfare Department of Delhi.
- **Processing Time:** Decisions are typically taken within 45 days.

## Mode of Payment
The monthly grant is remitted quarterly into the Bank account through the ECS system of RBI.
